Services

Four services, one coordinated practice.

Whichever stage your project is at, the same team carries it through — so the structural model, the drawings, and the site advice always agree with each other.

For new construction

Structural design & analysis

A full engineering package for ground-up builds — from the first load calculation through to drawings your contractor can price and build from.

Typical timeline: 3–6 weeks, depending on scale.

What's included

  • Dead, live, wind & seismic load calculations (IS 875, IS 1893)
  • Foundation & framing drawings
  • RCC or steel detailing as required
  • Material & specification schedules
  • Coordination meetings with your architect
02 — Existing buildings

For additions & retrofits

Renovation & retrofitting

Working around a structure that's already standing — additions, adaptive reuse, and seismic strengthening of older buildings.

Typical timeline: 2–5 weeks.

What's included

  • Existing-structure condition survey
  • Retrofit scheme: jacketing, bracing, or base strengthening
  • Updated structural drawings reflecting existing conditions
  • Contractor coordination during the retrofit
03 — Before you commit

For due diligence

Site & foundation assessment

Before you commit to a design — or a land purchase — we walk the site and tell you what the ground will and won't support.

Typical timeline: 1–2 weeks.

What's included

  • Site walk-through & existing condition notes
  • Review of soil investigation reports
  • Foundation type recommendation
  • Preliminary feasibility note for your records
04 — During construction

For sites under construction

Construction administration

Structural oversight while the building goes up, so what gets built matches what was drawn — and questions get answered fast.

Typical timeline: For the duration of construction.

What's included

  • Scheduled site visits
  • RFI response, usually within 24 hours
  • Shop drawing & submittal review
  • Close-out certification

Questions

A few things people usually ask

Do you work directly with homeowners, or only through architects?

Both. About a third of our work comes directly from homeowners and small developers; the rest is in partnership with an architect who brings us in for the structural side.

Can you review a soil report someone else commissioned?

Yes — send it over with your site address and we can fold it into a site assessment without re-ordering the investigation.

Do you handle the municipal approval submission?

We prepare drawings in the format your local authority requires and can attend approval meetings, though the formal submission is typically filed by your architect of record.

Not sure which service fits?

Tell us what stage your project is at — we'll point you to the right starting point.
